# A Very Brief Introduction to Obidi's Theory of Entropicity (ToE): Core Pillars, Redefinitions of Physical Phenomena, and Philosophy 


Obidi's Theory of Entropicity (ToE) is a theoretical physics and philosophical framework developed by independent researcher John Onimisi Obidi that proposes entropy is the fundamental, causal substrate of physical reality rather than a secondary statistical measurement of disorder. In this framework, spacetime, gravity, quantum mechanics, and matter are not fundamental primitives, but rather emergent "macro-shadows" generated by a dynamic, universal entropic field. [1, 2, 3] 

## The Core Pillars of the Framework
The theory operates on a complete "ontological inversion" of standard 20th-century physics: [4] 
* Entropy as a Fundamental Field (S(x)): Instead of computing entropy after identifying matter and geometry, the theory treats entropy as a continuous, dynamic, and local physical field. [4, 5] 
* The Obidi Action Principle (OAP): A foundational variational principle analogous to the Einstein-Hilbert action in general relativity. It integrates classical and quantum information geometry—specifically leveraging the [Fisher–Rao and Fubini–Study metrics]
* (https://medium.com/@jonimisiobidi/how-information-geometry-is-transformed-into-the-physical-geometry-of-spacetime-in-obidis-theory-16bf772d2412)—to optimize informational and entropic flows. [6, 7, 8, 9] 
* The Master Entropic Equation (MEE): The governing, non-linear field equation of ToE. Serving as the entropic equivalent to Einstein's field equations, it models the mathematical balancing of entropic production, causality, and spectral coherence. [10, 11, 12, 13] 
* The Obidi Curvature Invariant (OCI): Derived mathematically as ln(2), this represents the ultimate unit of "entropic cost" or distinguishability. The universe only registers a state or physical separation once the entropic curvature divergence passes this specific threshold. [10, 14] 

## Redefining Physical Phenemona
Rather than treating established laws of physics as separate axioms, the framework uses the Obidi Correspondence Principle (OCP) to argue that standard laws emerge naturally under conventional physical regimes: [2, 11] 

* Gravity as Entropic Pressure: Gravity is stripped of its status as a fundamental force; it is reinterpreted as spatial gradients or pressure generated within the entropic field as systems naturally seek higher entropic equilibrium. [10, 15] 
* The Speed of Light (c): The speed of light is not an arbitrary constant but is derived as the absolute maximum throughput rate at which the entropic field can reconfigure information. [3, 16] 
* The "No-Rush" Theorem: Posits that physical state transformations cannot occur instantaneously. Because building up the necessary entropic curvature divergence requires a finite duration, instantaneous quantum collapse or "spooky action" is replaced by finite entropic transitions. [10, 17] 
* The Obidi Probability Law: Challenges standard quantum mechanics by dividing the wavefunction into a visible coherent sector and an invisible entropic sector. Measurement is viewed as an irreversible transfer of amplitude between these sectors, making probability an objective, observer-independent physical field quantity rather than a mere measure of belief. [18] 

## Metaphysics and Ontodynamics
Beyond strict mathematical physics, John Onimisi Obidi bridges his scientific program with process metaphysics through a sub-discipline he titles Ontodynamics—the study of existence itself as stabilized entropic motion. Within this philosophical layer, physical "Being" represents an entropic gradient successfully persisting within a localized boundary, while "Becoming" is the irreversible redistribution of that entropy. 

The framework has also been speculatively extended to quantify consciousness through an Internal Self-Referential Entropy (SRE) Index, framing the brain as an entropy-processing engine. [19, 20, 21]

# Scholium 

John Onimisi Obidi’s "Theory of Entropicity" (ToE) is an audacious theoretical framework that recasts entropy not as a byproduct of disorder, but as the fundamental physical field and foundational substrate of the universe. It attempts to unify quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, and gravity from first principles.

# The Core Pillars

## Ontological Primacy of Entropy: 
Obidi’s first conjecture proposes that everything we experience—spacetime, mass, energy, and information—is simply an emergent, macro-shadow of an underlying, dynamic entropic field.

## The Obidi Action & MEE: 
The mathematical core of the theory relies on a variational principle called the Obidi Action. From this, the Master Entropic Equation (MEE) is derived, functioning as an entropic analogue to Einstein's Field Equations in General Relativity.

## The "No-Rush" Theorem: 
Rather than treating the speed of light (c) as an arbitrary geometric constant, ToE defines it as the maximum throughput rate at which the entropic field can reorganize its information.

# Philosophical & Conceptual Implications
## Ontodynamics: 
Obidi establishes a new philosophical discipline termed "Ontodynamics"—the study of existence itself as stabilized entropic motion. In this view, "Being" (existence) is the persistence of entropic gradients, while "Becoming" (change) is irreversible entropic redistribution.

## Decoupling from the Observer: 
Unlike some interpretations of quantum mechanics that center privileged observers, ToE frames consciousness as an emergent phenomenon—defined as systems running specialized, internal entropy-processing loops.

## Bridging Frameworks: 
Obidi's research serves as a meta-framework that encompasses other entropic and emergent gravity concepts, notably aligning with the research of Erik Verlinde, Ted Jacobson, and Ginestra Bianconi.
