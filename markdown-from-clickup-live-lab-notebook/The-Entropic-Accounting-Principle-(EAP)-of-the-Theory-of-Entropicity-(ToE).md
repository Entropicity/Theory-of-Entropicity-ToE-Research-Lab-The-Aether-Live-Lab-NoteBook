# The Entropic Accounting Principle (EAP) of the Theory of Entropicity (ToE)

In the context of John Onimisi Obidi’s Theory of Entropicity (ToE), the concept of Entropic Accounting was introduced by John Onimisi Obidi himself as a foundational mechanism of quantum mechanics and relativity. [1, 2] 
Rather than a financial or economic principle, Entropic Accounting in Obidi's framework is a fundamental physical law dictates how reality constructs itself. [2, 3, 4, 5] 
## The Role of Entropic Accounting in ToE
In Obidi's theoretical physics framework, the universe is treated as a continuous entropic computation where entropy is the primary, generative field of reality (rather than a passive measure of disorder). Within this framework, Entropic Accounting operates on several core premises: [3, 6, 7] 

* **The Entropic Time/Transmission/Transformation Limit (ETL):** Entropic Accounting mandates that physical reality and structure must be "earned" through a finite interval of entropic maturation. [2, 3] 
* **The Rejection of Instantaneity:** Because everything must be "accounted for" through the reconfiguration of the entropic field, quantum entanglement or measurement cannot occur instantaneously. This is used to resolve Einstein's famous dilemma regarding "spooky action at a distance." [2, 3, 8] 
* **Derivation of the Speed of Light (c):** The speed of light is not viewed as an arbitrary, fundamental constant, but rather the absolute maximum throughput and "bookkeeping" rate at which the entropic field can reconfigure information. [3, 9] 
* **Relativistic Resistance:** Relativistic phenomena—such as time dilation and mass increase—are interpreted as the physical manifestation of "entropic resistance" when a system attempts to reconfigure itself faster than the entropic field allows. [2, 3]
