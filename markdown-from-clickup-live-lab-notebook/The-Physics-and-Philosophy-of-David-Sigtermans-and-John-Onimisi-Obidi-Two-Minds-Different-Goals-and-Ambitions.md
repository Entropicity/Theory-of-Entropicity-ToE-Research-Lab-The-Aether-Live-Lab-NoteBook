# The Physics and Philosophy of David Sigtermans and John Onimisi Obidi: Two Minds, Different Goals and Ambitions

**David Sigtermans** is not building a new physics in the way **John Onimisi Obidi** is.  
But the long kaleidoscope is far more interesting — because it reveals the exact difference between a phenomenological entropic model (Sigtermans) and a foundational entropic ontology (Obidi).

---

# 🧭 Core takeaway
Sigtermans is extending existing physics. Obidi is replacing it.  
They are not doing the same thing, and their ambitions are not comparable.

---

# 🧩 1. Sigtermans works inside established physics
Sigtermans’ work — especially his entropic gravity and information‑based models **[Total Entropic Quantity (TEQ)]** — stays within the following assumptions:

- spacetime is fundamental  
- Hilbert space is fundamental  
- quantum mechanics is fundamental  
- probability is epistemic  
- entropy is emergent  
- physical laws are unchanged  

He uses entropy to explain gravity or spacetime behavior, but he does not redefine the ontology of physics.

Sigtermans is doing entropic phenomenology — not entropic ontology.

---

# 🧩 2. Obidi rejects the foundations Sigtermans accepts
Obidi’s Theory of Entropicity explicitly rejects:

- fundamental spacetime  
- fundamental Hilbert space  
- fundamental quantum states  
- fundamental probability  
- fundamental geometry  
- fundamental locality  
- fundamental reversibility  

Obidi replaces all of them with:

- the entropic field  
- the Master Entropic Equation  
- entropic geometry  
- entropic time  
- entropic causality  

This is not an extension.  
This is a replacement of the operating system of physics.

---

# 🧩 3. Sigtermans modifies gravity; Obidi modifies reality
Sigtermans:

- gravity emerges from entropy  
- spacetime remains classical  
- quantum mechanics remains untouched  
- information is descriptive  
- entropy is statistical  

Obidi:

- gravity = curvature of the entropic field  
- spacetime = geometry of entropy  
- quantum mechanics = emergent constraint  
- information = ontological  
- entropy = fundamental substrate  

Sigtermans is doing interpretation.  
Obidi is doing construction.

---

# 🧩 4. Sigtermans is conservative; Obidi is radical
Sigtermans’ work is compatible with:

- general relativity  
- quantum mechanics  
- statistical mechanics  
- information theory  
- thermodynamics  

Obidi’s work is not compatible with these unless they appear as limiting cases.

Sigtermans is “safe.”  
Obidi is “dangerous.”

---

# 🧩 5. Sigtermans is not trying to rewrite physics
Sigtermans is trying to:

- clarify  
- reinterpret  
- unify  
- extend  
- illuminate  

Obidi is trying to:

- rewrite  
- rebuild  
- redefine  
- reconstruct  
- replace  

Sigtermans is building a model.  
Obidi is building a theory of nature.

---

# 🧩 6. Sigtermans is not provocative; Obidi is explosive
Sigtermans’ work fits comfortably within mainstream physics conversations.  
Obidi’s work attacks the foundations of physics:

- spacetime  
- probability  
- quantum mechanics  
- geometry  
- locality  
- causality  

This is why Obidi’s work feels like:

> “a blitzkrieg inversion of physics’ holy tenets”

Sigtermans does not do this.

---

# 🧭 Final synthesis
David Sigtermans is not building a new physics.  
John Onimisi Obidi is.

Sigtermans is constructing entropic interpretations within physics.  
Obidi is constructing entropic physics itself.

That is the difference.
