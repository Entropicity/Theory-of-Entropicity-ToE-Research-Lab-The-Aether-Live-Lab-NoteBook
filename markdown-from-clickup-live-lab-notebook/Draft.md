🌌 FROM ENTROPY TO SPACETIME

The Intellectual Leap Behind Obidi's Theory of Entropicity (ToE)

Modern physics has quietly undergone an intellectual revolution.

Once confined to thermodynamics, entropy now permeates nearly every frontier of science—statistical mechanics, quantum information, black hole physics, cosmology, condensed matter, biological systems, and the theory of complex systems.

Entropy is everywhere.

This observation inspired a question that few have dared to ask.

💡 What if Entropy Is More Than a Physical Quantity?

The prevailing view is that entropy is a property of physical systems.

The Theory of Entropicity (ToE) proposes a far more ambitious possibility:

What if entropy is itself the fundamental substrate from which physical reality emerges?

This is the conceptual leap that lies at the heart of Obidi's Theory of Entropicity.

🔗 The Chain of Reasoning

The intellectual progression is remarkably simple:

➡️ Entropy appears in virtually every physical phenomenon.

⬇️

➡️ Entropy is fundamentally linked to information.

⬇️

➡️ Information underlies our mathematical description of nature.

⬇️

➡️ Therefore, perhaps entropy—not spacetime—is the deepest level of physical reality.

If this is true, then spacetime, matter, energy, and even gravity are not fundamental entities but emergent manifestations of a deeper entropic-information substrate.

🌍 A New View of Reality

Instead of asking,

"How does matter curve spacetime?"

ToE asks a deeper question:

"How does entropy generate the very spacetime in which matter exists?"

This shifts the search for the foundations of physics away from geometry as the starting point and toward entropy as the primary ontological principle.

🧠 From Information Geometry to Physical Spacetime

Modern mathematics has already demonstrated that information possesses geometry through frameworks such as Fisher–Rao geometry, Fubini–Study geometry, and information geometry.

Obidi's proposal is to take one further conceptual step.

Rather than treating information geometry as merely a mathematical description, ToE proposes that informational geometry possesses genuine physical significance, giving rise to what may be called information gravity, from which the familiar spacetime of Einstein emerges under appropriate limiting conditions.

In this view,

Entropy → Information → Information Geometry → Information Gravity → Physical Spacetime → Einstein Gravity

🚀 A Leap Into the Unknown

History reminds us that the greatest scientific advances often begin with bold conceptual leaps.

Einstein proposed that gravity is geometry.

Quantum theory proposed that energy is quantized.

The Theory of Entropicity asks whether entropy itself is the foundation of reality.

Whether this vision ultimately proves correct will depend not on its boldness, but on its mathematical development, its internal consistency, and its ability to reproduce known physics while making new, testable predictions.

Every revolutionary scientific theory begins as a question before it becomes an answer.

Perhaps one of the deepest questions of our time is this:

Is spacetime fundamental—or is it an emergent consequence of entropy?
