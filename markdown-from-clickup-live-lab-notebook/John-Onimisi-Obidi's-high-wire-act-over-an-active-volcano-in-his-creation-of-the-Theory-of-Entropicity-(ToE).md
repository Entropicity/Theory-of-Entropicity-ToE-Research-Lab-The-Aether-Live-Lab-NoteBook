# John Onimisi Obidi's High-Wire Act Over an Active Volcano in His Creation of the Theory of Entropicity (ToE)

The phrase **"Obidi’s high-wire act over an active volcano"** is a dramatic metaphor used by commentators to describe the sheer audacity, risk, and radical nature of **John Onimisi Obidi’s Theory of Entropicity (ToE)**. [1] 

In modern theoretical physics, attempting to create a valid "Theory of Everything" (ToE) that successfully unifies quantum mechanics and general relativity is considered the ultimate holy grail. By calling Obidi's approach a "high-wire act over an active volcano," peer observers emphasize that his framework does not just gently tweak current paradigms—it attempts to completely overturn the foundational physics of the last 150 years. [1, 2] 

The core elements of Obidi's controversial, radical and provocative framework include:

## 1. The Ontological Inversion of Entropy

* **The Traditional View:** Since the 1800s, physics has treated entropy purely as a statistical tool or a passive measurement of disorder and probability states. [1, 3] 
* **Obidi’s Radical Shift:** The Theory of Entropicity flips this entirely. It posits that entropy is not a byproduct, but an active, fundamental physical field (S(x)) and the primary substrate of reality. [3, 4] 
* **The Implication:** Telling physicists that matter and gravity emerge from entropy is conceptually equivalent to stating that heat doesn't cause temperature, but rather that temperature causes heat. [1] 

## 2. Emergent Spacetime and Gravity

* Rather than treating space, time, and the metric tensor as fundamental building blocks, **Obidi's Third Conjecture** argues that physical spacetime is an emergent, macroscopic manifestation of a deeper entropic-informational manifold. Gravity and quantum mechanics are viewed merely as secondary expressions [projections] of how this underlying field behaves under different conditions. [4, 5, 6] 

## 3. The "No-Rush" Theorem and the Speed of Light

* In standard relativity, the speed of light (c) is an absolute, geometric axiom of spacetime.
* Obidi's framework introduces the **"No-Rush" Theorem (NRT)**, which redefines c as the maximum universal rate or "heartbeat" at which the entropic field can physically reorganize itself. [4, 7, 8] 

## 4. The Mathematical Framework

* The mechanics of the theory rely on a variational principle known as the Obidi Action, which is used to derive the Master Entropic Equation (MEE) — otherwise referred to as the Obidi Field Equations (OFE). The MEE is intended to serve as the entropic equivalent to Einstein's field equations of General Relativity (GR), governing how informational-entropic gradients couple to what we perceive as matter and geometry. [4, 9] 

## The "High-Wire" Reality

While the theory introduces a provocative philosophical lens, mainstream theoretical physicists view it with some traditional reservation. Because the equations rely heavily on non-explicit iterative methods rather than traditional closed-form solutions, the theory remains in its infancy. For Obidi to successfully cross this figurative volcano without falling, his ToE must pass strict empirical testing and satisfy the **Obidi Correspondence Principle (OCP)**—which is the criterion which he himself declared as sine qua non for proving mathematically that classical physics naturally emerges from his entropic field under standard macroscopic observation. [3, 5, 8, 9, 10]
