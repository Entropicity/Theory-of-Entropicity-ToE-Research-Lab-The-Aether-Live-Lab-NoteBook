# How Obidi Derived Einstein's Relativistic Kinematics of Time Dilation, Length Contraction, and Mass-Energy Increase from the Theory of Entropicity (ToE): Principles and Methodology 

**John Onimisi Obidi** derived **Einstein's Relativistic Kinematics** from the **Theory of Entropicity (ToE)** primarily using The Entropic Accounting Principle (EAP) alongside the No-Rush Theorem (NRT). [1, 2, 3] 
Rather than taking the speed of light ($c$) as an axiomatic geometric constant, Obidi's framework reinterprets relativistic kinematics as an emergent phenomenon governed by the dynamics of a universal entropic field. [4, 5] 

## 1. The Entropic Accounting Principle (EAP)
The Entropic Accounting Principle replaces Minkowski space geometry as the baseline explanation for physical motion. [2] 

* The Core Insight: EAP dictates that when an object moves, its physical parameters must undergo a sequence of local entropic updates.
* Emergent Limits: Because information and configuration changes cannot happen instantaneously, the finite flow and redistribution of entropy impose a hard limit on how fast updates can cascade through the field. This fundamental limit of entropic rearrangement is what we physically measure as the speed of light ($c$). [3, 5, 6] 

## 2. The No-Rush Theorem (NRT)
The No-Rush Theorem provides the explicit mathematical constraint that forbids superluminal (faster-than-light) processing. [4] 

* The Law: It establishes that no entropic system can undergo instantaneous reconfiguration; every localized "update" requires a non-zero, finite temporal interval. [3] 
* Kinematics as Resistance: Under the NRT, traditional Einsteinian kinematics—such as time dilation, length contraction, and mass increase—are not primitive properties of spacetime. Instead, they emerge as physical expressions of entropic resistance. As an object's speed increases, the entropic field requires more processing overhead to update the system's states, causing time to slow down (dilation) and mass to appear to increase from the observer's frame. [1, 4, 7, 8, 9] 

## 3. The Obidi Action Principle (OAP)
To make these principles mathematically concrete, Obidi applies the Obidi Action Principle. This variational framework defines a Lagrangian for the entropic field. By treating the homogeneous and isotropic entropic field dynamically, the math yields Einstein's special relativity equations as emergent, thermodynamic constraints rather than rigid, unexplainable background postures. [3, 9, 10, 11]
